Rare New Testament Greek lemmas not covered in your course, ordered by descending NT frequency in buckets of 200 (sub‑groups of 25). These are not counted toward course‑wide progress, but they do appear in the analytics for whatever you have selected.

NT Book Vocab

Every lexeme that appears in each book of the New Testament, grouped into sets of 50 by how often it occurs in that book. User guide

How to use this app

An unofficial study aid for Mounce, Basics of Biblical Greek (BBG3). Flip a card, mark how confident you were, and the app schedules what returns next. Vocab from Paul Denisowski's BBG3 lists; grammar and paradigm order follow Mounce's overheads.

Home screen

Choose session — Mounce's four Parts, cumulative milestones, manual chapters, supplemental paradigm decks, and Advanced vocabulary buckets.

Mode strip picks what kind of card you study:

  • Vocab — Greek ↔ English flashcards.
  • Grammar — multiple-choice parsing and concept drills (Noun Rules, Five Rules of Contraction / μι Verbs, Master Verb Chart).
  • Parsing — walk the parse of a real inflected form one step at a time (aspect, tense, voice, mood, person, number, case, gender). Each step is chapter-gated so values appear only after Mounce introduces them. Wrong answers show your parse vs correct with the Greek form; stem-recall dead-ends redirect to the matching FLIP set.
  • Translate — graded NT reader. Each chapter lists every verse whose lemmas are all in your cumulative vocab through that chapter (Robinson-Pierpont 2005 Byzantine text, joined on Strong's). Tap a verse for a literal gloss.
  • Memorization — separate page with full paradigm tables; toolbar hides Greek, English, or notes for self-testing.

Progress — analytics overlay: streak, level, heatmap, course completion, achievements, study-time ledger, plus separate Selected and Total breakdowns. The Paradigm parsing section logs per-step accuracy with a tappable bucket-of-20 chart per row.

Advanced review settings

All toggles persist across sessions.

  • Font — Serif (Gentium Plus) or Sans (Noto Sans), both bundled locally. Text size — Medium / Large / X-Large.
  • Shuffle, Required only, Eng → Gk, Spaced review.
  • Hard review — drill only vocab you've missed 10+ times and are still under 40% on.
  • Daily archive reset — clear archived (Easy) cards around 5 AM so unspaced decks start fresh each day.
  • Split vocab/grammar selection — keep separate chapter selections for the two modes.
  • Self-check (grammar) — hide the answer until you tap to reveal.
  • Parsing-step toggles (parsing mode) — turn any of the eight dimensions off, or open the nested "Exclude … values" panel to skip individual values (e.g. drill aorist only). Each label notes the chapter where the value enters.
  • Optional paradigm extensions (parsing) — include or exclude imperatives, subjunctives, infinitives, participles, 3rd-person forms, and future / perfect tense forms by category.
  • Reshuffle / Reset deck / Reset required / Reset stats. The spaced-reset prompt also offers "Set all to now" (keep progress, make every card due) and can be scoped to required cards only.

How spaced repetition works

  • ✗ Hard — back within minutes; ease drops.
  • ~ Uncertain — back at ~half the previous interval (1h–1wk).
  • ✓ Easy — advances ~1 → 3 → 7 → 14 days, then grows.

With Spaced review off, Hard / Uncertain shuffle the card back into the active pile; Easy archives it. The deck reshuffles when the pass ends; archived cards stay until you tap ↻ Reset or pick a new session. Use ↶ Undo to reverse your last spaced mark.

Progress tools and notice

Progress tools export/import your progress as JSON. Study aid notice re-opens the disclaimer.

Keyboard shortcuts (while studying)

Flip: Space / Enter. Navigate: ← → (or ↑ ↓). Mark: 1 Hard, 2 Uncertain, 3 Easy. Quick: R=Hard, K=Easy. Grammar / Parsing: 1–4 picks an answer, 0/5=pass, ←=Undo.

Grammar self-check: Space / Enter reveals · 1 = I had it, 2 = Needs review.

Tips

  • Be honest with Hard / Uncertain / Easy — the schedule only helps if it reflects what you actually knew.
  • Use Parsing after a paradigm is fresh in your head — it tests recognition of inflected forms, not table memory.
  • Switch to Memorization for side-by-side paradigm review.
  • Theme, font, and text size are in the header / advanced settings.
Before you begin

Please read before using this app

This study tool was made with AI by a student. It is unofficial and may contain mistakes, omissions, or misleading answers.

Always check vocabulary, parsing, grammar explanations, and quiz content against your course content, instructor guidance, and official materials.

This app is meant to help with review and memorization, not to replace assigned coursework or authoritative sources.

Quick start: flip a card, then mark Hard, Uncertain, or Easy.

Reset known

What would you like to reset?

Sets each form's per-form tally back to 0/2, so its parsing dots read as unseen again and any forms hidden by “Exclude known morphs” come back into the drill. The in-card “Last 20 attempts” rollup is kept — use “Clear parsing stats” to wipe everything.

Current paradigm only clears just the current paradigm.

All paradigms clears every paradigm you've drilled.

Reset spaced review

What would you like to reset?

Set all to now keeps your SRS progress (streaks, intervals, ease) but makes every card in this deck due immediately, so you can review them all right away.

Smooth schedule keeps all progress and just levels the review calendar: when many cards pile up on the same future day (e.g. after completing a reset), it pulls some of them to earlier days so roughly the same number come due each day. Cards due within the next 3 days are left alone, and nothing is ever pushed later.

Reset progress clears all SRS scheduling and progress for this deck. Pass history and unspaced marks are kept.

Reset stats

What would you like to reset?

Reset stats clears all saved study stats, marks, spaced-review scheduling, achievements, and study-time history. Your settings (theme, fonts, profile, disclaimer) are kept.

Reset to start wipes everything and returns the app to its initial launch state — stats, settings, theme, fonts, profile, and the study-aid disclaimer are all cleared, and the page will reload.

Reset deck

Reset this flip deck?

Brings every archived card back into rotation and reshuffles the deck. Your spaced-review scheduling, intervals, confidence history, and pass history are kept.

Setting

Mounce variant

Mounce BBG3 build

Vocabulary. Sourced verbatim from Paul Denisowski's BBG3 per-chapter lists (chapters 4 and 6–36; chapters 1–3, 5, 15, and 26 introduce no new lemmas).

Grammar & paradigm order. Aligned to Mounce's published overheads: Noun Rules 1–8, Five Rules of Contraction, Three Uses of αὐτός, Master Case-Ending Chart, Master Verb Chart, participle morphemes (ντ, μενο/η, οτ), Five Rules of μι Verbs, and the four classes of conditional sentences.

Sessions. Replaced the lecture-week presets with Mounce's four Parts (Introduction · Noun System · Indicative Verb · Nonindicative & μι) and three cumulative milestones (through Ch 14, through Ch 25, all 36 chapters).
