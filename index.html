User guide

How to use this app

An unofficial study aid for William D. Mounce, Basics of Biblical Greek (BBG3). Flip cards, mark how confident you felt, and the app schedules what comes back next. Vocabulary is sourced from Paul Denisowski's BBG3 chapter lists; grammar concepts and paradigm order follow Mounce's published overheads.

Home screen tabs

Choose session. Pre-built sessions (the four BBG Parts plus cumulative-through-Ch milestones), manual chapter selection, supplemental paradigm decks, and an Advanced vocabulary section with rare NT lemmas grouped in buckets of 200.

Start studying. Begins (or resumes) a session for whatever you have selected.

Vocab / Grammar / Translate / Memorization. The mode strip switches what kind of card you study or jumps to a separate page:

  • Vocab — Greek headword on the front, English gloss on the back.
  • Grammar — multiple‑choice parsing and concept drills aligned to Mounce's Noun Rules, Five Rules of Contraction, Five Rules of μι Verbs, and the Master Verb Chart.
  • Translate — short author‑written Greek phrases per chapter, restricted to vocabulary and grammar introduced through that point. (The Mounce variant ships without NT‑verse curation.)
  • Memorization — separate page with full paradigm reference tables (article, nouns, pronouns, adjectives, verbs by tense). Use the toolbar to hide Greek, English, or notes for self‑testing.

Progress. Opens an analytics overlay with your study streak, level/title, daily activity heatmap, course‑completion bars, achievements, cumulative active study time, session history, and per‑deck vocabulary and grammar progress.

Advanced review settings

Tucked under Advanced review settings on the home screen. All toggles persist across sessions.

  • Shuffle — randomize card order in the current deck.
  • Required only — hide optional / supplemental words and study only course‑required vocabulary. On by default.
  • Eng → Gk — flip the prompt direction (English shown first, you recall the Greek). Works in both vocab and grammar modes.
  • Spaced review — turn spaced repetition on or off. On by default.
  • Self‑check (grammar mode only) — hide the answer until you tap to reveal, then rate yourself.
  • Reshuffle — re‑order whatever cards are currently due/eligible without changing your progress.
  • Reset deck — clear marks and progress for the currently selected sets only.
  • Reset stats — wipe all study statistics globally. There is no undo.

How spaced repetition works

Each card has its own schedule. When you mark a card the app adjusts both when it returns and an ease factor that scales future intervals:

  • ✗ Hard — comes back within minutes, drops ease and steps the card back one stage.
  • ~ Uncertain — comes back at roughly half its previous interval (capped between 1 hour and 1 week), small ease decrease.
  • ✓ Easy — advances to the next ramp step (~1 → 3 → 7 → 14 days, then growing), increases ease, and recent accuracy further multiplies the gap.

With Spaced review off, the deck behaves as a straight cycle: cards you mark Easy fall out of rotation until the deck is recycled, and Hard/Uncertain cards keep coming back inside the same pass. Use the ↶ Undo button under the card to reverse your last mark in spaced mode.

Keyboard shortcuts (while studying)

Flip card: Space / Enter

Navigate: ← → (or ↑ ↓)

Mark confidence: 1 Hard, 2 Uncertain, 3 Easy

Quick marks: R = Hard, K = Easy

Grammar options: 1–4 chooses an answer, 0 / 5 = "I don't know" (pass), ← = Undo last answer

Tips

  • Be honest with Hard / Uncertain / Easy — the schedule only helps if it reflects what you actually knew.
  • Switch to the Memorization page when you want to study paradigm tables side‑by‑side rather than one card at a time.
  • The Translate drills are short author‑written sentences that use only vocab/grammar through that chapter — the easiest bridge from flashcards to real syntax.
  • Theme (System / Dark / Light) is in the top‑right of the header.

Mounce variant

Mounce BBG3 build

Vocabulary. Sourced verbatim from Paul Denisowski's BBG3 per-chapter lists (chapters 4 and 6–36; chapters 1–3, 5, 15, and 26 introduce no new lemmas).

Grammar & paradigm order. Aligned to Mounce's published overheads: Noun Rules 1–8, Five Rules of Contraction, Three Uses of αὐτός, Master Case-Ending Chart, Master Verb Chart, participle morphemes (ντ, μενο/η, οτ), Five Rules of μι Verbs, and the four classes of conditional sentences.

Sessions. Replaced the lecture-week presets with Mounce's four Parts (Introduction · Noun System · Indicative Verb · Nonindicative & μι) and three cumulative milestones (through Ch 14, through Ch 25, all 36 chapters).

You can re-open the study aid notice any time from the Study aid notice button on the home screen.
